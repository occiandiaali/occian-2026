# There's Nothing New

There's nothing new under the Sun.
That's the saying. A variation, I suppose, of the more verbose: The more things change, the more they remain the same. We see it in fashion, human conflicts, romantic relationships, and software.
In the current AI age, those who build software for a living have been scared shitless by the fear of being "replaced by AI". The sporadic layoffs in the tech sector over the last 3 years have not helped. But, haven't we seen this already?

## We've Been Here Before

<intro>In the late 1990s the likes of [Adobe Dreamweaver](https://www.adobe.com/ng/) by **Macromedia**, and FrontPage by **Microsoft** must have seemed like a death knell for website developers when they first arrived. The former was targeted at professional web developers, while the latter was meant for beginners and non-techies. And to think of it, the premise was pretty much the same as we see today. After all, Dreamweaver meant that even less-skilled developers could suddenly produce good quality websites, and FrontPage made it possible for every Joe, June, and Janet to also lay claim to the "Web Developer" sobriquet - somewhat like how today, everyone is a "digital creator". But did web developers get replaced by tools like Dreamweaver and FrontPage? No. And the thing is, they weren't meant to replace them.</intro>

### A Tool Is A Tool

Those products, and software in general, are just tools. And tools simply help make work easier, or faster, or sharper. But they still need to be used by tools-persons. Sure, a number of web developers must have seen a hit to its income when those tools were introduced because the tools meant the capability was no longer held by a select few. And what did those who lost their positions do? They adjusted. Instead of seeing Dreamweaver or FrontPage as enemies of progress, they embraced them as helpers.
Maybe that's what this entire AI reality is also all about. Pretty much. If what we've seen in the past is anything to go by.

## Ruby Programming

Ruby is a general-purpose programming language designed with an emphasis on programming productivity and simplicity. Development of the language began in the mid-1990s by Yukihiro "Matz" Matsumoto in Japan. See the [Wikipedia page](<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ruby_(programming_language)>) for more. The language was all the rage from around the mid-2000s uptil 2015, favoured by start-ups like Twitter especially.

## How The Mighty Have Fallen

There was a time when it was a language like PHP that had the continual tag of:
Is it dead? on its forehead, but it now looks like Ruby has officially acquired the tag.

**Ruby on Rails** is really the only/main talking point of the language.
**Language libraries** haven't followed the path of something like Python.
**Magic methods** what used to be a plus, is now a minus to new devs.

## A Ranking below

The TIOBE Programming Community index is an indicator of the popularity of programming languages (Fun-fact, TIOBE stands for The Importance Of Being Earnest - [a play by Oscar Wilde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Importance_of_Being_Earnest)), and since I first came across it Ruby had been in the top 20.
Not anymore. The [TIOBE rankings for June, 2026](https://www.tiobe.com/tiobe-index/) places
the Ruby programming language at number 33! That's lower than Objective-C, Perl, Scratch, or Visual Basic (languages you'd wonder who uses). Does such a low ranking actually mean its
fallen out of favour, or isn't still used? Well, consider that TypeScript is ranked even lower, at number 37! Make of that what you will.

## Lessons Learned

- Keep dependencies minimal.
- Write content in Markdown for portability.
- Use plain HTML/CSS/JS for longevity.
